Tutorial

Information on how to use our application,
Tips on how to get the most out of it, and help on a few advanced settings.
For more information about our application, visit our about page.

Instructions

To start exercising, do the following:
  1. Start Application (Red Circle):
    Click on the green "Start Camera" button and allow the website to use your camera when prompted.
  1. Start Exercise (Red Circle):
    When the application finishes loading, select an exercise from the drop down list, and then click "Start Exercise".
  1. Camera Position (Orange Boxes):
    Position your camera according to the instructions in the alert box. More information on camera placement can be found in the "Tips" section.
  2. Rep Feedback (Green Box):
    Start doing the exercise. Rep count and instructions to improve your form will be seen on screen. It will also be read out by a narrator.
  3. Technical Issues (Blue Box):
    Any issues encountered by the application, such as being unable to see you fully, will be displayed here.
  4. Fatigue Detection (Yellow Box):
    If you are performing an exercise where the application can see your face (front squats), feedback based on sentimental analysis will be displayed here.
  5. End Exercise (Red Circle):
    When you are done, cick on "End Exercise" to see a summary of your reps.
  1. Summary of Feedback (Green Box):
    A summary of your reps and the feedback given will be displayed here.
  2. Show Feedback Log (Blue Box):
    The full log of feedback for each rep can by displayed by clicking this link.
  3. About Errors (Yellow Boxes):
    More information on the feedback given can be found by clicking "About Errors" or by clicking "Form" in the navigation bar.
  4. Start Another Exercise (Red Circle):
    To resume exercising, select an exercise from the drop down list, and then click "Start Exercise".

Tips

  1. For 'Right Side' exercises, the camera should be set up facing your right side, as seen in the pictures above.
  2. Camera should be at table height for squats and floor height for push-ups.
  3. Camera angle should be vertical. For squats, the camera should be able to see your feet, as seen in the pictures above.
  4. Examples of camera views include the images above and the videos taken in the "Form" page.
  5. For best training results, please time each rep at about 3-4s.
  6. For more information about our application, visit our about page.

Advanced Settings

  1. Toggle Text-To-Speech (Green Box):
    Disable or enable narrator to read out rep feedback.
  2. Toggle Beginner or Expert (Blue Box):
    Beginner mode advices users to exercise within their limit, Expert mode advices users to exercise near or close to failure.
  3. Flip Camera (Yellow Box):
    Changes active camera on devices with multiple cameras. Only supported on some browsers and devices.
